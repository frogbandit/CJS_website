Columbia Japan Society was founded in order to introduce aspects of Japanese culture to members of the Columbia community. In the past years, we have held annual events such as Sushi Night, Curry in a Hurry, Movie Night, and Matsuri (Japanese Festival), as well as new events each year.

You do NOT need to be Japanese to join CJS – all you need is an interest in anything Japanese! We are actively looking for new members and creative event ideas, so let us know if you would like to be part of the CJS family.

JAPAN IN NEW YORK SERIES (featured in WEAI Soundings)
  • WHAT: Book-Off at Bryant Park!
  • WHERE: meeting at Columbia Gates on 116th and Broadway
  • WHEN: Saturday, March 7th, meeting at 11:00 AM sharp

Have you ever imagined what it would be like to be in a bookstore in Japan? Have you ever wanted to see rows and rows of your favorite Japanese manga lining the walls and shelves or to see your favorite Japanese celebrities gracing the covers of Japanese pop culture magazines? How, you ask? Experience Japan in New York! Come with CJS as we venture off into midtown and visit Kinokuniya and Book-Off, two bookstores commonly found all over Japan. Find your favorite Japanese classics such as The Tale of Genji by Lady Murasaki, or even books by your favorite American authors for as little as $1! We'll top our day off with some delicious Japanese cream puffs, onigiri, or obento (Japanese Lunch Boxes) at Café Zaiya.

Price: from $1 (books), ~$5 (desserts)
