For more information on learning Japanese at Columbia, chat clubs to practice your Japanese, and study abroad programs, go to Columbia University's Japanese Language Program's website.

For language exchanges and fun soirees with members of the Japanese community and interested-in-Japan community in Teacher's college, go to Teacher's College Friends of Japan

To discuss Japanese issues with graduate students from Japan, go to Columbia University School of International and Public Affairs' Nihon Benkyo Kai's (NBK) website.

If you are interested in learning taiko (Japanese drumming), check out the Taiko Society at Columbia University's Teachers College.

For information regarding lectures on Japanese politics, history, and the like, access Columbia University's Weatherhead East Asian Institute's website.

Another source of great lectures and Japanese culture events is Columbia University's Donald Keene Center.

For information on the Japan student activities across New York universities, go to the New York Japanese Student Association

For resources on internships with Japanese organizations and corporations, go to P-Style
